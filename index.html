Chemical kill vs. Mechanical kill

All our products use the same technique, mixing crystalised silica dioxide particles in a de-ionised water-based solution, creating a chemically inert solution that can be applied harmlessly to any surface.


When dried, the application results in a new formed surface that is only 4nm in size. Most common germs are known to be 50-100nm in size.


This formed layer is made of crystalline structures, or so called needles, that penetrate the cell wall of the biohazard when trying to attach, resulting in a process we call a ‘mechanical kill’.

This mechanical kill differs from the usual chemical kill processes that has historically been used to combat viruses, bacteria, and fungi.
